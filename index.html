A City Rooted in Heritage

Tangub City is a component city in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines, located in the northern coast of Mindanao. It sits along the beautiful shores of Panguil Bay, offering breathtaking views and a rich maritime heritage.

Known as the "Rice Granary of Misamis Occidental," Tangub City has long been a vital agricultural hub. Its fertile plains and hardworking people have made it one of the most productive rice-producing areas in the region.

The city became a component city by virtue of Republic Act No. 8300 on June 6, 1997, converting the municipality of Tangub into the City of Tangub. It retains its vibrant cultural identity while progressing toward modern development.
